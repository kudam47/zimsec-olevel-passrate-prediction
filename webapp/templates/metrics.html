Academic Metric Explanations
R² (Coefficient of Determination)

R² measures the proportion of variance in O-Level pass rates explained by the model's predictors. A value of {{ metrics.r2 }} means the model explains {{ (metrics.r2 * 100) | round(1) }}% of the variance in district pass rates. Values above 0.85 indicate excellent predictive power.

RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error)

RMSE of {{ metrics.rmse }} means the model's predictions are, on average, within {{ metrics.rmse }} percentage points of the actual pass rate. This penalises larger errors more heavily than MAE, making it sensitive to outlier predictions.

MAE (Mean Absolute Error)

MAE of {{ metrics.mae }} represents the average absolute prediction error. Unlike RMSE, MAE treats all errors equally, providing a more interpretable measure of typical prediction accuracy without amplifying outlier errors.

Cross-Validation

5-fold cross-validation yielded a mean R² of {{ metrics.cv_mean }} (±{{ metrics.cv_std }}). The low standard deviation confirms the model generalises consistently across different data subsets, reducing overfitting risk.
